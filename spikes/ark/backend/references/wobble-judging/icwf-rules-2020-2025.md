# International Competitive Wobbling Federation (ICWF)

## Official Scoring Reference — Season 14

### Overview

Competitive Wobbling is a precision balance sport where athletes ("Wobblers")
stand on a regulation Wobble Platform (the "Disc") and perform controlled
oscillations scored by a panel of three certified judges.

### The Disc

- Diameter: 60cm
- Surface: cork-topped birch plywood
- Pivot: single titanium hemisphere, 4cm radius
- The Disc must be re-certified every 3 competitions

### Scoring Categories (max 10.0 each)

**1. Amplitude (AMP)** How far the Wobbler displaces from vertical. Measured in
degrees.

- 0-5°: "Micro wobble" (scores 1-3)
- 5-15°: "Standard arc" (scores 4-6)
- 15-25°: "Deep wobble" (scores 7-9)
- 25°+: "Danger arc" (scores 9-10, but only if recovered)
- NOTE: A Danger Arc that results in a Splort scores 0.0 in AMP

**2. Frequency (FRQ)** Oscillations per 30-second scoring window.

- Optimal range: 2.5-3.5 Hz ("The Sweet Zone")
- Below 2.0 Hz: "Slug pace" — deduction of 1.5 points
- Above 4.0 Hz: "Buzzing" — impressive but penalized 0.5 for instability
- Judges reward consistent frequency over peak frequency

**3. Symmetry (SYM)** Equal displacement left and right. Measured as a ratio
(1.0 = perfect).

- Ratio 0.95-1.0: "Mirror grade" (9-10)
- Ratio 0.85-0.95: "Lean bias" (6-8)
- Ratio below 0.85: "Drift" (1-5)
- A signature lean (intentional asymmetry) is allowed ONLY in Freestyle rounds,
  not in Technical

**4. The Gleam (GLM)** The subjective, ineffable quality of a wobble's beauty.
This is what separates champions from technicians.

- Judges consider: smoothness of transitions, confidence, showmanship, crowd
  response, and "flow state" indicators
- The Gleam is NEVER awarded above 8.0 in Technical rounds
- A perfect 10.0 Gleam has been awarded only 3 times in ICWF history

### Deductions

- **Splort**: Complete loss of balance. Automatic 0.0 in AMP, plus 2.0 deduction
  from total score. A recovered Splort (catching yourself within 1 second)
  reduces the deduction to 0.5.
- **Disc Departure**: Both feet leave the Disc. Immediate disqualification.
- **Anchor Grip**: Bending knees past 90°. 1.0 deduction — this is considered
  "cheating gravity."
- **Vocal Wobbling**: Making sounds during a Technical round. 0.5 deduction.
  Permitted in Freestyle.

### Round Types

**Technical Round**: 3 x 30-second windows. No music, no costumes. Pure wobble
form. Gleam capped at 8.0. Judged strictly on form.

**Freestyle Round**: 1 x 90-second window. Music permitted. Costumes encouraged.
Signature leans allowed. Full Gleam range available. Crowd response factors into
Gleam score.

### Historic Note

The legendary Wobbler "Steady" Elara Voss holds the all-time record: AMP 9.2,
FRQ 9.8, SYM 10.0, GLM 9.7 = Total 38.7 (Season 11 Finals). Her signature move,
"The Pendulum Pause" (a momentary freeze at maximum amplitude before reversing),
has been widely imitated but never matched.
