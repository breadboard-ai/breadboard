# ICWF PRESS RELEASE — FOR IMMEDIATE DISTRIBUTION

**INTERNATIONAL COMPETITIVE WOBBLING FEDERATION** **Season 15 Announcement —
January 2026**

The ICWF Board of Governors is pleased to announce the most significant rule
update in the Federation's history. After two years of consultation with
athletes, judges, and fans, the following changes take effect for Season 15.

## Duo Wobbling Arrives

The headline addition: **Duo Wobbling** joins the competition calendar. Two
athletes will compete simultaneously on adjacent Discs, scored as a pair. A
fifth scoring category, **Resonance (RES)**, has been created specifically for
this format. Resonance measures synchronisation quality, with the pinnacle being
"Counter-Wobble" — identical frequency but 180° out of phase.

"This is the biggest evolution since we introduced Freestyle in Season 4," said
ICWF President Margaux Tenner. "Duo Wobbling demands a completely new kind of
athleticism."

Solo events are unaffected. Resonance does not apply to solo scoring.

## Technical Gleam Cap Adjustment

Following extensive feedback from the judging community, the Gleam (GLM) cap in
Technical rounds has been raised from 8.0 to 9.0. The Board acknowledges that
elite Technical Wobblers have been systematically under-rewarded for artistry
within form.

## New Weight Division

The Ultra class (100kg+) will split into **Ultra (100-120kg)** and a new **Titan
class (120kg+)**, colloquially "Glaciers." Titan-class athletes will compete on
wider 70cm Discs for safety. The Board consulted with sports physicians on this
decision.

## Additional Rule Clarifications

- A new explicit deduction for **Platform Manipulation** (-1.5) targets the
  "Stomp Drift" technique that caused controversy in Season 14.
- The Recovered Splort window extends from 1.0 to 1.5 seconds.
- Athletes in their 10th+ consecutive season receive a Legacy Bonus of +0.3
  applied after deductions.
